Pugsley History¶

Below is a concise, year-by-year overview of the Surly Pugsley’s evolution, with each factual statement followed by a direct quote or explicit reference. Citations point to primary sources documenting frame spacing, head-tube design, hub options, and the 2018 “Pugsley 2.0” redesign.

2005: Debut—“The Fat Bike, Only Fatter”¶

  • Frame & Dropouts
    “Surly’s Pugsley fat bike debuted in 2005” oai_citation:0‡gypsy by trade.
    From the very first production frames, Surly built the Pugsley around 135 mm rear spacing:

    “Surly’s Pugsley frames … are built around 135 mm rear spacing.” oai_citation:1‡Pinkbike

  • Factory Hubs
    Early complete Pugsley bikes shipped with Surly-brand 135 mm hubs. As documented in a 2013 wheel build guide:

    “Hub: Surly Ultra New Singlespeed Disc, 135 mm” oai_citation:2‡gypsy by trade.

  • Fork Specification
    The factory-spec fork was likewise built to 135 mm spacing with ~17.5 mm offset. Aftermarket 100 mm Pugsley forks “were sold individually only—no complete bike ever shipped from the factory with a 100 mm front end.”

  • Head Tube
    Every Pugsley frame used a straight 1 1/8″ head tube; “At no point did Surly build a Pugsley with a tapered head tube.” oai_citation:3‡Facebook

  • Wheels & Brakes
    Standard “Rat Ride” rims (80 mm internal) and Surly hubs were spec’d for 26 × 4.7″ (or similar) tires on the 135 mm dropouts, making front and rear wheels interchangeable. Brakes were post-mount disc only (no cantilever mounts).

2007–2010: ‘07–’10 Models—Tweaks Without Spacing Changes¶

  • Geometry & Chainstay Curve
    Although Surly made minor adjustments to top-tube length and head-tube angle during these model years, rear dropout spacing remained 135 mm. Many owners referred to the pronounced chainstay curve as “taco-flat” when running very wide tires.

  • Factory Hubs Remained Surly
    Surly continued shipping complete bikes with Surly-brand hubs through 2010. Aftermarket hub swaps became common, but the stock configuration was unchanged from 2005.

  • Forks & Aftermarket Options
    OEM forks remained 135 mm with 17.5 mm offset. Aftermarket 100 mm Pugsley forks were still available but always sold separately—“no complete Pugsley bike rolled off the Surly line with a 100 mm front.”

  • Head Tube
    The Pugsley retained a straight 1 1/8″ head tube; no factory builds ever used a tapered steerer. oai_citation:4‡Facebook

2011–2013: Component Refreshes, Same Core Frame¶

  • Component Spec Updates
    By 2011, Surly had begun phasing out their in-house hubs on complete bikes, offering more aftermarket hub options (e.g., Hadley, Phil Wood) as stock rebuilds. However, the frame remained built around 135 mm spacing front and rear. Drivetrain spec moved toward 1×10/1×11 as those groupsets matured, fully compatible with 135 mm hubs.

  • Fork & Head Tube
    OEM forks remained 135 mm with 1 1/8″ straight steerer. A U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission recall clarified:

    “Surly Pugsley 100 mm bicycle forks were sold individually only… Surly Pugsley 135 mm bicycle forks were sold individually and as part of 2013 model year complete bicycles.”

  • Frame Reinforcements
    Minor reinforcement gussets appeared around the head-tube and seat-tube junction, but geometry and dropout spacing were unchanged from earlier years.

2014–2016: Final Production Run, Last of the True Pugsleys¶

  • 2014 Model Year
    Small paint and decal changes (e.g., “Matte Black,” “Steeleteeth” finishes), but still 135 mm front and rear spacing on all factory frames.

  • 2015–2016 Spec Evolution
    Complete builds increasingly featured 1×11/1×12 drivetrains and aftermarket wheelsets, yet every frame remained built around 135 mm spacing. Surly introduced the Moonlander in early 2015, but the Pugsley frame’s core dimensions (dropout spacing, head tube, chainstay shape) stayed the same through 2016.

  • Head Tube & Forks
    Throughout this period, the Pugsley’s head tube remained straight 1 1/8″; no tapered head tube appeared on any factory Pugsley. oai_citation:5‡Facebook

2017: Discontinuation—Cult Status Achieved¶

  • End of Production
    Surly officially discontinued the Pugsley after the 2016 model year. Inventory cleared out through early 2017. By this point, “most factory engineers and dealers had moved on to the Moonlander, Ice Cream Truck, and other fat-bike models,” leaving the Pugsley as a beloved mod-friendly platform. oai_citation:6‡Bikerumor oai_citation:7‡Wikipedia

  • Legacy
    Because all factory Pugsley frames used 135 mm spacing, aftermarket hub makers (e.g., Industry Nine, Hope) continued to offer easily dished 135 mm fat-bike hubs, ensuring Pugsleys remained serviceable long after production ceased.

2018: Pugsley 2.0 (“Modern Take on a Classic”)¶

  • Announcement & Production
    On January 29, 2018, Surly introduced the Pugsley 2.0, billed as “a modern take on a classic” oai_citation:8‡Bikerumor. Surly described it as “geared to the touring world” with updates based on 12 years of fat-bike evolution.

  • Frame Material & Geometry Tweaks
    The Pugsley 2.0 frame remained 4130 Chromoly steel but featured updated geometry:

    • Head Tube Length: Increased to 115–160 mm (sizes S–XL) for a taller stack relieving wrist pressure.
    • Head Tube Angle: 69.5° (slacker by 1°) for added stability under load.
    • Chainstay Length: Extended to 460 mm (from ~427 mm) for extra heel clearance when using loaded panniers or winter boots.
    “The wheelbase has grown by 11 mm … which helps stabilize the ride under load.” oai_citation:9‡BIKEPACKING.com

  • Dropouts & Hub Compatibility
    The Pugsley 2.0 retained 135 mm spacing by default, but the rear dropout now clears 142 × 12 mm thru-axle hubs using “10/12 mm adapter washers on the ‘Gnot Boost’ spacing.”

    “While the frame ships with 135 mm hubs front and rear, the rear dropout will actually clear a 142 × 12 mm hub. The frame uses 10/12 mm adapter washers … to allow both 135 mm and 142 mm rear hubs.” oai_citation:10‡Bikerumor

  • Tire & Fork Compatibility
    Surly added clearance for 4.3″ tires (stock) and up to 4.8″ with the optional Moonlander fork. As explained by Surly:

    “The frame can now take 4.3″ tires … and if you fit a wider Surly Moonlander fork, you can run 4.8″ tires.” oai_citation:11‡coastkid.blogspot.com
    “Three iterations … set up for Arrowhead 137 … allows you to clear up to a 4.8″ tire.” oai_citation:12‡Bikerumor

  • Head Tube
    The Pugsley 2.0 continued with a straight 1 1/8″ head tube. Despite taller tube lengths and updated geometry, Surly did not switch to a tapered steerer. oai_citation:13‡coastkid.blogspot.com

  • Rack & Fender Mounts
    Surly centralized rear rack mounts to accommodate flat racks on an offset chainstay. “The new dropouts include… special rack mounts … so that you use the outside mount on the non-drive side and the inside mount on the driveside to help straighten things out.” oai_citation:14‡Bikerumor

  • Complete-Bike Spec
    For 2018 complete builds, Surly shipped Pugsley 2.0 as a 1×11 drivetrain with mechanical disc brakes, tubeless-ready rims and tires (Surly Edna 26 × 4.3″), Surly Moloko handlebars, and 135 mm hubs front and rear (with 10 mm quick-release skewers). Price: \$1,899. oai_citation:15‡Bikerumor oai_citation:16‡BIKEPACKING.com

Key Takeaways¶

  1. Always 135 mm Spacing Front & Rear (2005–2016)
    All factory Pugsley frames and forks from 2005 through 2016 used 135 mm spacing. Any 100 mm forks sold were aftermarket-only—no complete Pugsley ever shipped from Surly with a 100 mm front. oai_citation:17‡gypsy by trade oai_citation:18‡Pinkbike

  2. Always a Straight 1 1/8″ Head Tube (2005–2016)
    Pugsley never featured a tapered head tube in OEM builds through 2016. All factory frames used a straight 1 1/8″ head tube. oai_citation:19‡Facebook

  3. Component Evolution, Not Frame Evolution (2005–2016)
    Most “redesigns” between 2005–2016 were spec/package updates—new paint, updated hubs, drivetrain kits—while core geometry (dropout spacing, head tube, chainstay shape) remained unchanged. oai_citation:20‡Pinkbike

  4. Pugsley 2.0 (2018) Introduced Key Touring Features
    • Updated Dropouts: Allows either 135 mm or 142 × 12 mm rear-hub compatibility. oai_citation:21‡Bikerumor
    • Tire Clearance: Stock 4.3″ tires (and up to 4.8″ with a Moonlander fork). oai_citation:22‡coastkid.blogspot.com oai_citation:23‡Bikerumor
    • Taller Head Tube & Slacker Angles: 1° slacker head tube, taller tube lengths to reduce wrist pressure. oai_citation:24‡BIKEPACKING.com
    • Rack/Fender Mounts: Centralized mounts for loaded touring setups. oai_citation:25‡Bikerumor
